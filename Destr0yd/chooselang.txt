									~ Choosing a language ~

Let's imagine that you are a beginner. You have learned that there are different programming languages. Maybe you are somewhere
read about the existence of such a wonderful language as Assembler, and if not Assembler,
It could be about the popular C language, Java, etc. Most likely, you have no idea where to start and why.
In this article I will explain everything to you BRIEFLY, but if you don’t have enough information, then Google it.

	(This introduction to the article is necessary so that it is clear for whom it is written)

Usually all Japanese are divided into high-level and low-level.

High-level ones - in which it is more convenient to write large programs (not complex ones) than in low-level ones.
Essentially, their authors make it easier for you to solve various problems by creating ready-made functions, structures, and syntax.
They have it clearer (if it's not Lisp, haha!).

Low-level (Assembler) -- usually used to solve complex (serious) problems. For example, writing
drivers, viruses. But writing some kind of utility with a GUI interface and buttons and images will be more difficult,
since everything will have to be written from scratch...

C++, C, Java and others are high-level (google them and read more about them).
Assembler is a low-level language (the only one, also google it).

Conclusion

1. Don't be afraid of difficulties.
2. Write in the language(s) you enjoy writing in, i.e. don't choose just the most popular ones.

And that's all. See you later!
